Date: 2026-06-01

Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript, "SurfPrior-GC: a reproducible framework for uncertainty-aware gastric cancer surface-target prioritization with GPI evidence-routing audit", for publication as a full-length article in Computational Biology and Chemistry.

The manuscript presents SurfPrior-GC, a reproducible bioinformatics framework for prioritizing gastric cancer surface-target hypotheses from public transcriptomic, protein/localization, topology, normal-risk, surfaceome, benchmark-control, and stability evidence. The contribution is computational and methods-forward: the workflow preserves frozen ranking snapshots, explicit score components, coarse unordered tiers, and documented limits for bulk RNA, tumor microenvironment signal, isoform ambiguity, HPA bulk IHC granularity, external benchmark interpretation, and absence of wet-lab validation.

The most transferable result is an auditable GPI evidence-routing quantification/correction. We quantify how incomplete routing of confirmed UniProt GPI-anchor evidence affects surfaceome universe construction and downstream ranking, then apply a source-wide universe-stage correction before final scoring. The correction restores 54 confirmed GPI-anchor genes to the ranked universe, increases confirmed GPI-anchor representation from 65 to 119 genes, shifts CEACAM5 from rank 120 to 12, shifts MSLN from rank 453 to 158, and produces a median rank improvement of 453 positions among common confirmed GPI-anchor genes. This is framed as quantification and correction of a reproducibility-relevant integration issue, not as a first discovery of GPI-anchored surface proteins.

The applied output is a coarse, hypothesis-generating Tier 1/2 candidate set for gastric cancer follow-up. External comparisons are used conservatively: the ranking shows moderate concordance with a Cancer Surfaceome Atlas pan-cancer GESP baseline and consistency with a contemporary gastric proteogenomic surface-target atlas, but matched-null analyses show that the gastric atlas overlap is not enriched beyond matched expectation and does not establish candidate-level experimental evidence.
