# CSBJ Cover Letter Draft

Date: 2026-06-01

Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript, "A reproducible framework for gastric cancer surface-target prioritization and GPI-anchor auditing", for publication as a Research Article in Computational and Structural Biotechnology Journal.

This manuscript presents a reproducible public-data framework for prioritizing gastric cancer surface-target hypotheses from transcriptomic, protein/localization, topology, normal-risk, surfaceome, benchmark-control, and stability evidence. The contribution is methods-forward rather than a claim of clinical target validation. The workflow preserves a frozen ranking history, explicit score components, coarse unordered tiers, and documented limitations for bulk RNA, tumor microenvironment signal, isoform ambiguity, HPA bulk IHC granularity, and the absence of wet-lab validation.

The most transferable result is an auditable GPI evidence-routing quantification/correction. We quantify how incomplete routing of confirmed UniProt GPI-anchor evidence affects surfaceome universe construction and downstream ranking, then apply a source-wide universe-stage correction before final scoring. This is framed as quantification and correction of a reproducibility-relevant integration issue, not as a first discovery of GPI-anchored surface proteins.

The applied output is a coarse, hypothesis-generating Tier 1/2 candidate set for gastric cancer follow-up. External comparisons are used conservatively: the ranking shows moderate concordance with a Cancer Surfaceome Atlas pan-cancer GESP baseline and consistency with a contemporary gastric proteogenomic surface-target atlas, but matched-null analyses show that the gastric atlas overlap is not independent validation of the ranking or any individual nomination.

The manuscript and repository are intended to be auditable. Before formal submission, the public repository URL and archival DOI will be inserted into the data/code availability statement, and the corresponding-author email will be updated to a durable address retained after graduation.

The material has not been published and is not under consideration elsewhere. The sole author has reviewed the manuscript and accepts responsibility for the content, including the declared use of OpenAI Codex for manuscript organization, language editing, and repository-oriented consistency checks.
